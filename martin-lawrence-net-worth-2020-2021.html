Early Life

Martin Lawrence was born as Martin Fitzgerald Lawrence in 1965 on 16th April to John Lawrence and Chlora Lawrence in Frankfurt, West Germany. Martin was only eight years old when his parents separated. Their separation forced his mother to get a job.

He was raised in Queens in New York City. Growing up, he changed several schooling jumping from Thomas G. Pullen School of Creative and Performing Arts, then Fairmont Heights High School, then Eleanor Roosevelt High School and Friendly High School. He has also learned boxing and participated in several matches during his school days. Fighting helped him in dealing with his parent’s divorce. He was even considered to become a professional boxer until one day someone gave him a broken eye, which led to him dropping off these plans.

Personal Life

Martin Lawrence married his first wife Patricia Southall in 1995, but the couple separated after two years in 1997. The couple had one daughter named Jasmine Page Lawrence. He married his second wife, Shamika Gibbs, in 1997, who he divorced in 2010. He again remarried her in 2010 but divorced her in 2012. The couple has two daughters together named Iyanna Faith Lawrence and Amara Trinity Lawrence.

Age, Height, and Weight

Career

Martin Lawrence started off his career in 1987 as a standup comedian. He is known to find humor in subjects like Observational comedy physical comedy, satire, blue comedy, Human sexuality, African-American, culture, racism, race, relations, and self-deprecation.

Martin Lawrence made his Hollywood debut in 1989 with ‘Do the Right Thing.’ Later he appeared in movies like ‘House Party,’ ‘A Thin Line Between Love and Hate,’ ‘Nothing to Lose,’ ‘Big Momma’s House,’ ‘Talkin’ Dirty After Dark,’ ‘Bad Boys,’ ‘What’s the Worst That Could Happen,’ ‘Death at a Funeral,’ ‘College Road Trip,’ and ‘Rebound.’

He has also appeared in Television shows like ‘What’s Happening Now!!’, ‘Love That Girl!’, ‘Def Comedy Jam,’ ‘Hammer, Slammer, & Slade,’ ‘Private Times,’ ‘Martin,’ ‘Partners,’ and ‘The Soul Man.’ Martin Lawrence has also made two comedy albums ‘Funk It,’ and ‘Martin Lawrence Live Talkin’ Shit.’

Awards & Achievements
