Coming back to the pupusa‒picture a thick, corn tortilla lovingly stuffed with oozing cheese, refried beans, or succulent meat fillings. The combination of flavours and textures within a pupusa is nothing short of extraordinary, making it an irresistible treat for food lovers.

Intrigued? Here’s the recipe!

Recipe: Cheesy Bean and Chorizo Pupusas

Ingredients

2 cups Masa Harina (Corn Flour)

1 ½ cups Warm Water

1 cup Refried Beans

½ cup Crumbled Chorizo

1 cup Shredded Mozzarella Cheese

Salt, to taste

Oil, for cooking

Method

  • In a mixing bowl, combine masa harina and warm water. Knead the mixture until it forms a smooth dough. Add a pinch of salt for flavour.
  • Divide the dough into golf ball-sized portions. Flatten each ball slightly in the palm of your hand.
  • Place a spoonful of refried beans, a sprinkle of crumbled chorizo, and a generous amount of shredded mozzarella cheese in the centre of one flattened dough ball.
  • Carefully fold the edges of the dough over the filling, sealing it well. Gently pat the ball back into a thick tortilla shape.
  • Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and add a drizzle of oil. Cook each pupusa for 2-3 minutes on each side or until golden brown and the cheese is melted.
  • Serve hot with your favourite salsa, guacamole, or curtido (a tangy cabbage slaw).
